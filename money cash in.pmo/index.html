Problem Statement

In Brunei, plastic pollution is a major environmental issue, with plastics being the most common waste found on beaches, rivers, and public areas.

Plastic waste is also a big concern because it is unable to decay and breaks into microplastics which harms the fish and corals.

Back in 2018, there was a clean up at Pelumpong Spit & Surrounding Waters. The waste collected from the area includes styrofoam, plastic bags, bottles and plastic packaging. Even in 2020 & 2024, at Pelong Rocks, they manage to collect a lot of plastics, and it is said that the plastics were tangled with marine life or caught in coral reefs.

*Pelumpong Spit is located at Brunei Muara District, Mukim Serasa.

Objectives

Our aim/goal is to:

  • Promote sustainable use of materials - materials are reused instead of thrown away.
  • Reduce litter and improper disposal - keeps public areas clean.
  • Encourage a lot of people to start recycling plastics and other recyclable materials.
  • To reduce waste going to landfills and ocean dumping.
  • To accomplish SDG 12 (Responsible consumption and production), 13 (Climate Action), and SDG 14 (Life below water).

Solutions

Since plastic is commonly found on beaches, rivers and public areas, our smart bins encourages people to throw plastics in bins instead of littering. It also reduces the need of large scale cleanups in the future as less plastic will be litter in the environment and stop waste from flowing into rivers and oceans protecting the fish, corals and other marine organisms.

The bin not only collects and stores plastics for recycling, but it also provides an incentive system where the weight of plastic waste is converted into money or rewards through QR code scanning.

This creates a behavioral change, as people are more motivated to recycle when there is a tangible benefit.

Key Features/Functions

This bin uses sensor and servo motor to open its automated cover, so that is easy for people to carry their plastics and just dump it in the bin.

And then the plastic will be weigh inside the bin by using load cell and HX711.

After it is being weigh, the total "KG" will be shown on a OLED screen, which then the total weight will be converted to currency (e.g. 0.5kg a 0.50 cents).

And lastly customer will scan the QR code to receive their money. But they will have to sign up by entering their phone number.
